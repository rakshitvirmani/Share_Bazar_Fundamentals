# Pitti Engineering Limited — Fundamental Analysis
**BSE: 513519 | NSE: PITTIENG | As of February 2026**

---

## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

Pitti Engineering Limited (formerly Pitti Laminations Limited), founded in 1983 by Shri Sharad B Pitti, is India's largest manufacturer and exporter of electrical steel laminations. Over four decades, the company has evolved from a specialist lamination maker into a comprehensive, vertically integrated engineering solutions provider for the rotating electrical equipment and industrial machinery industry.

The business operates across two principal verticals — **Rotating Electrical Equipment** (laminations, stator-rotor assemblies, copper built-up rotors, traction cores) and **Machined Components** (precision shafts, housings, gear cases, axle boxes, fabricated parts), supported by a dedicated **Foundry/Casting Division** (grey iron, SG iron, stator frames, end shields). This end-to-end integrated model — from raw material sourcing to finished subassemblies — is a significant moat, allowing Pitti to compete on quality, customisation, and delivery rather than just price.

**Six manufacturing facilities** across India:
- Kothur & Nandigaon, Telangana (core lamination)
- Macharam, Telangana (machined components)
- Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar (CSN), Maharashtra (mega plant — lamination & assemblies)
- Tumakuru, Karnataka (acquired: Pitti Industries Pvt Ltd — pumps & agricultural segment)
- Hosakote, Karnataka (acquired: Dakshin Foundry — castings, export-quality)

**FY2025 Consolidated Capacity (post-acquisitions):**
- Sheet Metal: 90,000 MT/annum (expanding to 1,08,000 MT post ₹150 cr capex)
- Machining: 6,48,000 machine hours (expanding to 7,20,000 hours)
- Casting: 18,600 MT (expanding to 24,600 MT)

## 2. INDUSTRY TAILWINDS — WHY THIS SECTOR MATTERS NOW

Pitti sits at the intersection of several of India's most powerful structural growth themes:

**Indian Railways Electrification & Expansion:** Over 98% of broad-gauge network electrified, deployment of 200+ Vande Bharat trains, 100 Amrit Bharat trains, development of 2,843 km of Dedicated Freight Corridors — all translate directly into demand for traction motors, alternators, and precision engineered components. Traction power is targeted to reach 10,000 MW by 2030.

**Data Centre Boom:** India co-location capacity reached 977 MW in FY2025, growing 105% YoY. As data centres densify, cooling motors and backup generators become mission-critical. Pitti supplies laminations and generator stator cores for this segment, which has grown from ~2% to ~4% of quarterly revenues in a single year.

**EV & Industrial Electrification:** The global EV motor market is projected to grow at 18.2% CAGR to ₹97,394 crore by 2029. FAME India and related incentives are expanding demand for PMSMs, BLDCs, and induction motor components across two- and three-wheelers and industrial drives.

**Renewable Energy:** Wind, hydro, and solar electrolysers require high-quality laminated cores and alternator components. The newly commissioned revarnishing line opens the hydro power segment — making Pitti the only commercial supplier in India of revarnished laminations for hydro and thermal power.

**Global Supply Chain Shift:** India is increasingly competitive vs China in precision electrical engineering. Pitti's customer list reads like an industrial blue-chip directory — ABB, Siemens, Alstom, Caterpillar, Komatsu, Wabtec, SKF, Voith, Nidec, CG Power, BHEL, Tata — providing strong export visibility.

## 7. STRATEGIC M&A — A TRANSFORMATIONAL FY25

FY2025 saw Pitti execute a series of transactions that fundamentally transformed the company:

**Acquisition of Bagadia Chaitra Industries Pvt Ltd (now Pitti Industries Pvt Ltd)** — May 2024: Specialist in electrical steel laminations, assemblies, and die-cast rotors. Located in Tumakuru, Karnataka. Opens the agricultural pump and appliance segment; adds South India manufacturing footprint.

**Acquisition of Dakshin Foundry Pvt Ltd** — July 2024: Located in Hosakote, Karnataka. Specialises in locomotive gear and axle housing castings. Adds export-quality global casting clients (especially European relationships), increases consolidated casting capacity to 18,600 MT, and strengthens backward integration.

**Merger of Pitti Castings Pvt Ltd (PCPL) and Pitti Rail & Engineering Components Ltd (PRECL) with Pitti Engineering** — effective April 2023, NCLT order October 2024: Vertical integration of castings and railway components. Eliminates inter-company costs, rationalises casting assets, and streamlines the group structure.

The net result: Pitti has moved from a company with a ₹1,000 crore revenue run rate and a single core capability (laminations) to an integrated ₹1,700–2,000 crore revenue enterprise with capabilities spanning lamination, assembly, machining, casting, and foundry — serving clients across 15+ industries.

## 8. KEY GROWTH DRIVERS

**Driver 1 — Indian Railways (structurally multi-year):** Railways & traction motors constitute ~32% of revenues. Vande Bharat, metro rail, dedicated freight corridors, and locomotive upgrades create a multi-year demand pipeline. Wabtec (multi-year contract worth ₹700+ crore) remains a flagship customer. Railway consolidation in the USA (the BNSF/UP discussion) is also a demand tailwind for Pitti's export traction business.

**Driver 2 — Data Centres (fastest growing, 0→4% in 12 months):** Pitti secured a second data centre platform with an existing customer in Q1 FY26. Each new platform can generate ₹20+ crores of recurring annual revenue at peak. With 25–30% sector growth expected over the next 12–18 months, this could be a 6–8% revenue segment by FY27.

**Driver 3 — Export Market Share Gain:** Exports are ~30% of revenues, with the US (9–10% of total revenues) and Mexico (significant contribution) as key markets. Indian lamination suppliers are increasingly preferred over Chinese suppliers given 30% China duties vs India's 25% advantage. Recent wins: Xylem (US castings), SKF (European castings), two new European customers in FY26.

**Driver 4 — Operating Leverage:** The company is at ~70% utilisation currently and expects Q4 FY26 to hit ~80%+ (optimum). Every incremental ton at high utilisation drops proportionally more to EBITDA. The 170 bps YoY margin expansion in Q1 FY26 is the first demonstration of this; management targets 16.5–17% EBITDA margin as steady state, with upside to 18%+ as casting and machining scale.

**Driver 5 — Hydrogen & Renewable Energy:** Newly commissioned revarnishing line makes Pitti the only Indian commercial supplier of revarnished laminations for hydro and thermal power. Electrolyser components for hydrogen fuel cells are actively being evaluated. These are high-value, high-margin add-ons.

**Driver 6 — Product Mix Upgrade:** Revenue share of machined components and castings (currently ~33% combined) is expanding. These businesses carry higher margins than basic laminations. As the mix shifts, blended EBITDA margins should structurally improve.

## 9. MANAGEMENT QUALITY ASSESSMENT

**Akshay S Pitti (MD & CEO):** Second-generation leader who has been transformational. Shifted the company from commodity laminations to engineered subassemblies, built the mega-plant at CSN, executed three M&A deals in FY24–25, and raised ₹360 crore QIP at a premium. Concall communication is candid and data-driven — acknowledges working capital stress openly, provides realistic guidance ranges, and is clear on the tariff risk.

**Track record of guidance delivery:** FY25 guidance of ~15% revenue growth was met and exceeded (20.9% standalone, 34.9% consolidated). FY26 guidance of 15% growth (given in Q1 FY26 call) is tracking to ~28% on consolidated basis — consistent pattern of under-promising and over-delivering.

**Management's four strategic priorities for FY26:** (1) Seamless integration of acquisitions, (2) Margin expansion to 16.5–17%, (3) Innovation in high-value products (R&D, hydrogen, data centres), (4) Capital discipline and return optimisation. All four are measurable and management is regularly accountable on concalls.

**Credit Rating:** Long-term bank loan upgraded to IND AA- (Stable), Short-term IND A1+ — a meaningful external validation of balance sheet quality.

| Inventory | ~₹500 crores (elevated; target ₹300–350 cr by FY27) |
| Working Capital Cycle | ~75 days (vs ~57 days prior year) |

**Key Balance Sheet Concerns:**
The working capital cycle stretched from 57 days to 75 days in FY26, driven by two factors: (1) Pitti stopped factoring export receivables (which was reducing reported sales but is now correctly reflected in the interest line), and (2) the company stocked up raw material (silicon steel) due to BIS import quality control disruptions on electrical steel from China/Japan/Korea — which temporarily constrained supply from April–September 2025.

The QIP proceeds of ₹360 crores (FY25) were deployed primarily to reduce borrowings (₹270 crores) and fund working capital. This brought net debt from ~₹760 crores (pre-QIP) to ₹439 crores by March 2025. However, working capital investments in FY26 have pushed net debt back to ~₹550 crores.

Management targets inventory reduction to ₹300–350 crores (from ₹500 crores) over the next 12 months as the raw material situation normalises, which should release ₹150–200 crores of working capital and meaningfully reduce net debt. This is a key near-term catalyst.

Using FY27E PAT of ₹175–210 crores:
- P/E on FY27E: ~19–23x

For a company growing revenues at 25–30% CAGR, expanding margins, with a structural multi-sector demand tailwind, and an improving balance sheet post-inventory normalisation, the FY27 valuation is compelling. The key re-rating trigger is inventory reduction and net debt reduction demonstrating the cash conversion quality of the business.

Comparable peers in the electrical engineering/components space (Bharat Bijlee, CG Power, BEML, ABB India) trade at 20–35x earnings. Pitti's complexity premium is earned through its vertical integration and export capabilities; its discount (vs large-cap peers) is justified by smaller scale and balance sheet leverage.

## 14. INVESTMENT PERSPECTIVE

**Bull Case:** Railway electrification and modernisation sustain 30%+ volume growth for 3–4 years. Data Centre segment hits 8–10% of revenues by FY28. Inventory normalises, releasing ₹200 crores of cash, reducing net debt to below ₹350 crores. EBITDA margin expands to 18–19% as machining and casting scale. Revenue crosses ₹2,500 crores in FY27 with PAT of ₹200+ crores. At 28x forward earnings, the stock has significant upside.

**Base Case:** Revenue reaches ₹1,950–2,000 crores in FY26 and ₹2,300 crores in FY27. EBITDA margin stabilises at 17–17.5%. PAT grows to ₹140–145 crores in FY26 and ₹175–190 crores in FY27. Net debt reduces gradually as working capital normalises. Stock likely to trade at 22–28x FY27 earnings.

**Bear Case:** US tariffs materially impact 10% of revenues, railway order flows disappoint, and inventory normalisation is delayed by another year. FY26 revenues hit only ₹1,800 crores with flat margins, and FY27 earnings disappoint at ₹130–150 crores. Elevated debt becomes a concern, with potential credit quality pressure.

**Key Catalysts to Watch:**
1. Q4 FY26 results — inventory levels and net debt trajectory
2. New data centre platform ramp-up
3. Hydrogen electrolyser component order announcement
4. Export customer wins (European accounts via Dakshin)
5. Working capital cycle normalisation from ₹500 cr inventory to target ₹300–350 cr
