## 1. Company Overview

Bluspring Enterprises Limited is India's nationally-scaled, integrated infrastructure services company. It was carved out of Quess Corp through a three-way demerger (Quess, Digitide, Bluspring) effective April 2025 and listed on June 11, 2025 at approximately Rs 89.

The company operates through category-leading brands across four business segments: Avon (Facility Management), Indya Foods (Food Services), Terrier (Security Services), Hofincons (Industrial O&M), and Vedang (Telecom Infrastructure). It also holds an investment in foundit, an AI-powered job search platform.

With 90,000+ employees across 28 states, 21 locations, and 1,000+ customers, Bluspring is one of very few pan-India, compliance-first infrastructure services players. This scale is a genuine differentiator in an industry still dominated by unorganized, non-compliant operators.

## 2. Business Segments Deep Dive

### 2.1 Facility & Food Services (~60% of Revenue)

This is the bread-and-butter segment. The Avon brand manages housekeeping, landscaping, pest control, and technical services across 7,000+ sites for 650+ customers. Indya Foods serves 180,000 meals daily across 90+ sites — hospitals, universities, industrial parks, and corporates.

**Q3 FY26 Performance:**
- Revenue: Rs 521 Cr (up 11% YoY, 1% QoQ)
- 9M Revenue: Rs 1,511 Cr (up 13% YoY)
- EBITDA Margin: 4.5% (up 50 bps QoQ)
- New contracts won in Q3: Rs 79 Cr ACV; Total 9M ACV: Rs 189 Cr
- Key client sectors: Education, Healthcare, Industrials, Commercial, BFSI

Management has been pruning low-margin accounts and replacing them with higher-quality deals — new housekeeping deals are winning at 150-200 bps higher margins vs. last year. A new central kitchen in Whitefield, Bengaluru is expected to go operational in Q4 FY26, expanding food services into corporate offices and GCCs.

The food business has clear seasonality — Q1 is weak (education institutions shut in June-July), Q2 recovers sharply, and Q3 (October-December) is the strongest. The company has also entered sports and leisure hospitality (exclusive partner for World Para Athletics Championship), which is a new growth vector.

### 2.2 Telecom & Industrial Services (~18% of Revenue)

This segment houses two sub-verticals:

**Telecom (Vedang):** A 26-year legacy brand managing 237,000 network nodes/month across all four major Indian telecom operators for network deployment, assurance, and tower infrastructure management. Vedang is a market leader in telecom active infrastructure services.

**Industrial (Hofincons):** A 47-year-old brand handling end-to-end O&M across ferrous, nonferrous, power, and green industries. The company is actively pivoting from traditional manpower supply to SLA-based, outcome-oriented O&M contracts — a meaningful margin improvement lever.

**Q3 FY26 Performance:**
- Revenue: Rs 151 Cr (up 2% YoY, down 3% QoQ)
- 9M Revenue: Rs 458 Cr (up 10% YoY)
- EBITDA Margin: 9.9% (up 160 bps QoQ) — highest margin segment
- Won a Rs 20 Cr ACV contract for consolidated electrical and instrument maintenance for a large manufacturer
- First overseas project deployed (50 resources outside India)

The telecom vertical has been soft since Q2 as network operators slowed 5G rollouts. Management expects a pickup in Q4. The strategic pivot is diversifying into solar EPC, satellite communications, and international projects. By Q1 FY27, Bluspring expects presence in two overseas geographies.

Management guidance: This segment should grow 12-15% YoY on a sustained basis.

### 2.3 Security Services (~20% of Revenue)

Operated under the Terrier brand, this segment provides man-guarding and electronic security across 2,200+ sites in 24 states with PSARA licenses. It has the fastest growth trajectory in the group.

**Q3 FY26 Performance:**
- Revenue: Rs 173 Cr (up 15% YoY, 3% QoQ)
- 9M Revenue: Rs 489 Cr (up 14% YoY)
- Headcount addition: 2,500+ guards over last 12 months (12% YoY growth)
- 50+ new logos added in FY26 so far
- 900+ open mandates in pipeline

Q3 EBITDA was muted due to one-time provisioning (Rs 75-80 lakh) on certain receivables and bonus billing adjustments. Excluding this, the segment is on a healthy trajectory. Management is investing in sourcing channels and has guided confidence in maintaining similar growth trends.

### 2.4 Foundit (Investment Vertical, ~2% of Revenue)

Foundit is an AI-powered job search platform — think of it as India's #2 player behind Naukri (InfoEdge). Bluspring holds approximately 70% of foundit.

**Q3 FY26 Performance:**
- Revenue: Rs 18 Cr (down 27% YoY, but 45% sales productivity increase over Q1)
- Product revamp completed — better UI/UX, improved search relevance, 25% reduction in site latency
- Cost base reduced from Rs 45 Cr/quarter (Q4 FY25) to Rs 30 Cr/quarter
- New CBO and CEO hired recently

This is the most controversial part of the Bluspring story. Revenue has been declining as the company invested heavily in product revamp, but management is confident of a revenue inflection starting Q4 FY26, targeting Rs 25-30 Cr/quarter. Breakeven is guided within 3 quarters from Q3 FY26 (i.e., by Q2 FY27). Total investment in foundit to date: ~Rs 285 Cr (including Rs 250 Cr historical + Rs 35 Cr incremental).

The bull case: At scale (30% CAGR post-breakeven), this could be a 20-30% EBITDA margin platform business. The bear case: It has been a cash drain, breakeven timelines have already slipped once (originally guided FY25-end, now pushed to Q2 FY27), and it is competing against a dominant incumbent (Naukri).

## 3. Financial Analysis

### 3.1 Revenue Trajectory (Excluding Foundit)

| Quarter | Revenue (Rs Cr) | YoY Growth | QoQ Growth |
|---------|-----------------|------------|------------|
| Q1 FY26 | 777 | 13% | -1% |
| Q2 FY26 | 837 | 14% | 8% |
| Q3 FY26 | 844 | 10% | 1% |
| 9M FY26 | 2,458 | 12% | - |

Revenue growth has been consistent at 10-14% YoY across all quarters since listing, driven by new contract wins. The sequential flattening in Q3 is due to telecom weakness and deliberate pruning of low-margin facility management accounts.

### 3.2 EBITDA and Margin Progression

| Quarter | EBITDA (Rs Cr) | EBITDA Margin | QoQ Margin Change |
|---------|---------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Q1 FY26 | 24 | 3.1% | - |
| Q2 FY26 | 29 | 3.5% | +40 bps |
| Q3 FY26 | 32 | 3.8% | +37 bps |
| 9M FY26 | 85 | 3.5% | - |

This is the key story — EBITDA margins are on a clear upward trajectory, from 3.1% at listing to 3.8% in Q3. 9M EBITDA is flat YoY because of strategic investments in sales leadership and team building post-demerger. Management has guided an exit rate of 4% by Q4 FY26 and a long-term target of 6% EBITDA margin by FY30.

The margin expansion roadmap rests on: higher-margin new contracts replacing old ones, operating leverage as revenue scales, the growing share of higher-margin segments (food, industrials), shift from manpower supply to outcome-based contracts, and digital adoption reducing costs.

### 3.3 PAT and EPS

| Quarter | Adjusted PAT (Rs Cr) | EPS (Rs) |
|---------|---------------------|----------|
| Q1 FY26 | 13 | 0.9 |
| Q2 FY26 | 16 | 1.1 |
| Q3 FY26 | 19 | 1.2 |

PAT grew 54% YoY and 14% QoQ in Q3. However, reported PAT in Q3 was negative (Rs -23 Cr) because of a one-time exceptional charge of Rs 29.8 Cr related to the new Labour Code (gratuity and leave encashment liabilities). Of this, Rs 20 Cr is recoverable from clients over the next 6 months through contract pass-through clauses, and Rs 9 Cr relates to core employees (non-recoverable). This is why the P/E ratio currently shows negative — it is a one-time distortion, not a structural profitability issue.

### 3.4 Balance Sheet Health

- Net Debt (Dec 31, 2025): Rs 107 Cr (down from Rs 136 Cr in Sep 2025, Rs 194 Cr peak)
- DSO: 98 days (down from 105 in Q2, normal range: 85-90 days)
- Guided OCF to EBITDA conversion: 50% on full-year basis
- Weighted average cost of debt: ~7.35% (down from ~8% at listing)
- Effective tax rate: 17-20%
- ROCE: Double-digit at blended operating level
- ROE (ex-foundit): ~6-7%, target 20% by FY30

The elevated DSO and debt in H1 FY26 were caused by novation of 1,000+ contracts from Quess to Bluspring post-demerger, which stalled billing and collections. This is now behind them — Q3 cash flows improved significantly, and management guides sub-Rs 100 Cr net debt by March 2026 end. Long-term debt guidance is below 1.5x EBITDA.

### 3.5 Cash Flow Concerns

H1 FY26 operating cash flow was negative (Rs -97 Cr) — the biggest red flag for bears. This was driven by three factors: novation delays disrupting billing cycles, receivables growing 25% vs. 14% revenue growth, and continued investment in foundit. Management is categorical that this normalizes in H2, with OCF breakeven at minimum by year-end and the 50% OCF/EBITDA target on a full-year basis.

## 4. Recent Developments

### 4.1 STEAG India Acquisition (March 19, 2026)

The most significant recent development. Bluspring is acquiring 100% of STEAG Energy Services India for Rs 180 Cr through its subsidiary BNHOPL. STEAG India is a leading power services company with:

- Rs 600 Cr+ annual revenue
- ~2,000 specialist professionals
- 7 GW of managed power assets
- 2,200 TPH of process steam supply capacity
- Operations in India, Botswana, Middle East
- Strong digital capabilities (Performance Monitoring, Predictive Analytics, Training Simulators)

This is a transformative deal for Bluspring's Industrial vertical. At Rs 180 Cr for Rs 600 Cr revenue, they are paying just 0.3x revenue — an optically cheap price, though the margin profile of STEAG India needs closer scrutiny. The deal is expected to close in 60-90 days.

If executed well, this acquisition nearly doubles Bluspring's Telecom & Industrial segment revenue overnight and significantly accelerates the international expansion strategy.

### 4.2 Ashish Dhawan Increasing Stake (March 23, 2026)

Ashish Dhawan, a well-known value investor and founder of Central Square Foundation, increased his stake from 4.96% to 6.10% through an open market purchase of 17 lakh shares on March 23, 2026. This is a notable signal — Dhawan is known for deep conviction, long-term bets.

### 4.3 New Labour Code Impact

The government notified new labour codes in November 2025, consolidating 29 separate laws into 4 comprehensive codes. Bluspring took a Rs 29.8 Cr one-time provision in Q3. Management views this as a structural tailwind for compliant, formalized employers like Bluspring, as it makes it increasingly difficult for principal employers to work with non-compliant vendors.

### 4.4 ELI Scheme (Employment Linked Incentive)

Effective from August 2025, this government scheme incentivizes formal employment creation. Bluspring expects to start recognizing benefits in Q4 FY26 or Q1 FY27 — could be a meaningful margin booster, though management will recognize on receipt basis only.

## 5. Management Assessment

**Kamal Pal Hoda - ED & CEO:** Comes across as an articulate, operationally focused leader on the earnings calls. He communicates strategy clearly — growing with discipline, not chasing revenue at the cost of margins. The "house of brands" approach (Avon, Terrier, Hofincons, Vedang, Indya Foods) shows thoughtful brand architecture.

**Prapul Sridhar - CFO:** Transparent in his disclosures. Proactively addressed the elephant in the room (Labour Code impact) on the Q3 call. Provides granular segment-level data. His approach to the exceptional item (recognizing the full liability upfront, being conservative on the revenue recognition from pass-throughs) is prudent.

**Arjun Sunil Makhecha - Company Secretary:** Handles compliance and filings.

**Promoter: Ajit Abraham Isaac** (also linked to Quess Corp), along with Fairbridge Capital (Mauritius) Ltd.

Overall management quality appears above-average for a company of this size. They are setting clear, measurable targets (6% EBITDA by FY30, 20% ROE, 3x GDP growth, sub-1.5x debt/EBITDA) and reporting progress against them each quarter. The risk is execution, not intent.

## 6. Competitive Positioning & Moat

### Industry Landscape

India's facility management market is estimated at USD 55-80 billion (2025) and growing at 7-14% CAGR depending on the segment. The industry is highly fragmented with significant unorganized presence, particularly in security and housekeeping.

### Key Competitors

- SIS Ltd (Security) — larger, listed peer in security services
- BVG India (Facility Management) — unlisted, strong in government FM
- ISS India, Sodexo India — MNC competitors in integrated FM
- Naukri/InfoEdge — dominant player in the job board space (vs. foundit)

### Bluspring's Competitive Advantages

1. National scale across 28 states with PSARA licenses in 24 states — very hard to replicate
2. Compliance-first approach benefits from regulatory tailwinds (Labour codes, ELI scheme)
3. Integrated service offering (FM + Food + Security + Industrial) allows cross-selling — only 3-4 customers currently buy all services
4. Legacy brand equity (Hofincons: 47 years, Vedang: 26 years, Avon and Terrier: 20+ years)
5. Diversified customer base — top 30 customers = only 49% revenue, 8 sectors each contribute >5%
6. Technology-enabled operations — digitized attendance for 90,000+ employees

The moat is not deep but it is wide — compliance at scale in a labor-intensive industry creates meaningful switching costs and barriers to entry. The Labour Code reforms structurally favor organized players.

## 7. Key Risks and Red Flags

### 7.1 Foundit — The Cash Drain
Foundit has consumed ~Rs 285 Cr of capital and continues to lose money. Breakeven has already been pushed back once. If the turnaround fails, this is dead weight on the balance sheet. At Rs 18 Cr/quarter revenue against Naukri's dominance, the path to meaningful profitability is long and uncertain.

### 7.2 Negative Operating Cash Flows (H1 FY26)
Rs -97 Cr OCF in H1 is concerning. While the novation story is credible, investors should watch Q4 cash flows closely to see if the normalization materializes.

### 7.3 Thin Margins in a Labor-Intensive Business
3.8% EBITDA margin means there is very little room for error. Any wage inflation that cannot be passed through, or a large client loss, can swing profitability sharply.

### 7.4 Telecom Cyclicality
The telecom business (high margin at ~10%) is inherently cyclical, tied to network rollout cycles. Post-5G, there could be a multi-year lull before 6G investments begin. Management is diversifying, but the near-term contribution is uncertain.

### 7.5 Execution Risk on STEAG Acquisition
Integrating a 2,000-person power services company while Bluspring is still stabilizing post-demerger is ambitious. The deal valuation (0.3x revenue) looks cheap, but could indicate underlying profitability or business quality issues with STEAG India.

### 7.6 Promoter Group Linkages
Promoter Ajit Abraham Isaac is associated with both Quess Corp and Bluspring. Cross-selling arrangements with Quess/Digitide carry related-party transaction risks that need monitoring.

### 7.7 Newly Listed Company
Bluspring has been listed for less than a year. There is limited independent financial track record. The historical financials were carved out from Quess, making comparability difficult.

### 7.8 Stock Price Down ~47% from Listing
The stock is trading near its 52-week low (~Rs 56) vs. listing price of ~Rs 89. This could indicate the market has found structural issues, or it could simply be a young stock finding its fair value after an initial premium.

## 8. Valuation Assessment

At a market cap of ~Rs 695 Cr, let us look at what the market is pricing in:

**On current (9M FY26 annualized) numbers:**
- Revenue (ex-foundit): ~Rs 3,275 Cr annualized → trading at 0.21x revenue
- EBITDA: ~Rs 113 Cr annualized → trading at ~6.1x EBITDA
- Adjusted PAT: ~Rs 64 Cr annualized → trading at ~10.9x earnings

**Post-STEAG (pro-forma):**
- Revenue could be ~Rs 3,875 Cr (adding Rs 600 Cr from STEAG) → 0.18x revenue
- If STEAG operates at even 5-6% EBITDA margins, that adds Rs 30-36 Cr → total EBITDA ~Rs 145 Cr → 4.8x EV/EBITDA

**FY27 estimates (if management delivers on guidance):**
- Revenue growth of 12-15% organically + STEAG contribution = Rs 4,200-4,500 Cr range
- EBITDA margins progressing toward 4.5-5% = Rs 190-225 Cr EBITDA
- At current market cap, this is 3.1-3.7x FY27E EV/EBITDA

For comparison, SIS Ltd trades at ~15-18x EV/EBITDA and Quess Corp trades at ~12-15x EV/EBITDA. Even applying a 50% discount for Bluspring's smaller size, thinner margins, and shorter track record, the current valuation looks undemanding.

**P/B of ~1x** is essentially saying the market values Bluspring at its book value — no premium for the business franchise, brands, or growth potential.

## 9. Investment Thesis Summary

### Bull Case (Target: Rs 80-100 over 12-18 months)

- Revenue scales to Rs 4,500+ Cr with STEAG integration and organic growth
- EBITDA margins expand from 3.8% to 5%+, driving EBITDA to Rs 200+ Cr
- Foundit hits breakeven and begins contributing positively
- Labour Code reforms and ELI scheme provide structural tailwinds
- Net debt reduces to sub-Rs 100 Cr, improving ROE trajectory
- Ashish Dhawan's stake increase signals smart money confidence
- Re-rating from 6x to 10-12x EV/EBITDA as the company establishes track record

### Bear Case (Downside: Rs 30-35)

- Foundit turnaround fails, requiring further capital infusion
- STEAG integration stumbles, margin dilutive rather than accretive
- Telecom rollout slowdown persists through FY27
- Cash flows don't normalize, forcing more debt
- Market continues to assign demerger-orphan discount

### Base Case (Target: Rs 65-75 over 12 months)

- Steady 12-15% revenue growth, margins reaching 4.5%
- STEAG integration goes through smoothly
- Foundit on track but not yet profitable
- Moderate re-rating as quarterly execution continues

## 11. Conclusion

Bluspring is a classic "demerger orphan" trading at what appears to be a deep value. The underlying operating businesses (FM, food, security, telecom, industrial) are growing at double digits with improving margins. The management team appears credible with clear articulation of strategy and targets.

However, this is not a sleep-well-at-night stock. The thin margins, foundit overhang, post-demerger teething issues (DSO, cash flows), and the ambitious STEAG acquisition introduce meaningful risks. The stock demands quarterly monitoring and active position management.

For an investor with a 2-3 year horizon and tolerance for near-term volatility, the risk-reward at current levels (Rs 46, ~6x EV/EBITDA on current numbers) tilts favorably. The STEAG acquisition at 0.3x revenue, if well-integrated, could be a significant value creator. The Labour Code tailwind is a genuine structural catalyst for formalized players.

I would not be a buyer of management's foundit optimism until actual revenue inflection is visible — that is a "show me" story, not a "trust me" story.
