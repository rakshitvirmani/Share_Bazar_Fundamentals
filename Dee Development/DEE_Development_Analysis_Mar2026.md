## 1. Business Model Overview

DEE Development Engineers is a specialized **process piping solutions company**, operating across three key segments:

- **Process Piping Manufacturing Solutions** (~87% of revenue) - custom pipe spools, skids, fittings
- **Heavy Fabrication** (~6-8%) - wind towers, structural fabrication
- **Power Generation** (non-core, ~6-8%) - biomass power plants in Punjab

Every item manufactured is **100% tailor-made to customer specifications** (built-to-print), making this a project-driven business with high entry barriers. The company serves marquee global and domestic clients including **Dow Chemical, ExxonMobil, Linde, GAIL, BHEL, L&T, Air Products, Reliance, Adani Petrochemicals, and Nayara Energy**. Approximately 50% of revenue comes from exports, serving clients across the US, Belgium, Canada, Czech Republic, Japan, Italy, and the Middle East.

**Revenue model:** The company operates on both a job-work basis (client supplies material) and a material-supply basis (DEE procures and converts). Per-kg realizations vary significantly based on metallurgy - carbon steel jobs yield lower realizations versus high-alloy steel (Inconel, Incoloy, stainless steel) which carry premium pricing.

## 5. Catalysts, DNA Shifts & Capex Coming Live

*This is the most critical section - identifying what can structurally re-rate the stock.*

### A. Thermal Power Piping - The Big Unlock

BHEL has **53 units** and L&T has **23 units** of 660-800 MW thermal power in their order books. Each unit requires ~INR 150-200 Cr of critical piping. BHEL plans to execute 10 boilers/year and outsource critical piping for 5 of those. DEE is the **dominant qualified player for P91/P92 high-alloy critical piping** - management says they see "not much competition" in this niche.

DEE is **L1 in key tenders** and expects INR 300-400 Cr of L1 orders to convert imminently. This is a multi-year, high-visibility order pipeline (3+ years) that can transform DEE's revenue mix. The company is preparing a detailed industry paper on the thermal power piping opportunity. Palwal facility is dedicated to this opportunity.

### A1. Thailand Subsidiary - Active Order Wins (BSE Filing: March 5, 2026)

DEE Piping Systems (Thailand) received a Letter of Intent for supply of HRSG piping for an international customer (Taiwan Project). Order value: ~Euro 1.9 million (INR 20 Crores), execution by May 2027. While small relative to the consolidated order book, this confirms the Thailand subsidiary is an active order-winning entity and validates DEE's international diversification beyond US/Europe/Middle East. Combined with INR 86 Cr of power sector inflow into Thailand in February, the subsidiary is showing meaningful traction.

### B. Anjar Facility - Full Commissioning (30K MTPA)

Phase 2 (additional 15K MTPA) was commissioned in Sep 2025, 2 months ahead of schedule. This is a game-changer because: (1) Dedicated oil & gas facility near Kandla port slashes logistics costs, (2) Modern automation and lean manufacturing drive operating leverage, (3) Separating oil & gas (Anjar) from power (Palwal) eliminates the complexity of running two different sector jobs in one plant. Management attributes 4-5% direct margin impact from overhead absorption alone as turnover scales 50%+.

**Update (March 12, 2026):** DEE has organized an Investor/Analyst Plant Visit at the new Anjar facility on March 20, 2026. This is a near-term sentiment catalyst — management is clearly confident enough in operational readiness to invite analysts for first-hand inspection. Plant visits for small/midcap companies often lead to initiation reports and estimate revisions. No UPSI will be shared per the filing.

### C. Seamless Pipe Plant - Backward Integration

INR 90 Cr capex for a 7K MTPA seamless pipe plant commencing Jan 2026. Manufactures thick-walled seamless pipes (up to 120mm) in alloy and stainless steel grades. Currently, DEE buys these pipes from third-party mills at market prices. By making them in-house, the supplier's margin becomes DEE's margin. Revenue potential: INR 200 Cr from the pipe plant at full utilization (2x asset turnover). The high-alloy, thick-wall nature means high value-add per kg.

### D. Green Hydrogen - Optionality Play

JV with International Clean-Tech Partner for modular hydrogen production systems. Molsieve Designs (majority-acquired subsidiary) holds proprietary technology for ultra-pure hydrogen purification (99.9999% purity). Targeting the 700-1,500 mm cube segment where competition is minimal. Currently pre-revenue - pilot plant expected within 3 months. This is pure optionality, not in current estimates.

### E. Diversification into Nuclear, Semiconductors, Pharma

Management has explicitly stated that post the current thermal power cycle, diversification focus will be on nuclear power piping (already NPCIL-qualified, in advanced discussions), semiconductor fab piping, and pharma piping. These are ultra-high-spec, high-margin applications perfectly aligned with DEE's capabilities. This represents the next 5-10 year growth leg.

### F. Power Segment Pivot - Biomass Pellet Plant

Biomass pellet manufacturing plant at Malwa using the same paddy straw feedstock. Trials ongoing, COD expected shortly. Should turn the power segment EBITDA-neutral in FY27 (from -INR 36 Cr currently). Removing this drag is equivalent to adding INR 36 Cr to the bottom line.

## 7. Investment Summary

DEE Development is at a genuine inflection point, and the March 2026 filings present a mixed but ultimately constructive picture. The order book has surged to INR 1,913 Cr (Feb 28) — 47% higher than the Dec 31 figure — with the thermal power piping cycle now confirmed as converting (INR 621 Cr of power inflow in February alone). The heavy capex phase is behind, capacity has doubled at Anjar, seamless pipe backward integration is going live, and the multi-year power piping opportunity is no longer "about to unlock" — it is unlocking.

However, the March 17 Strait of Hormuz disclosure (revised March 18) introduces a new risk overlay, though management has moved quickly to contain the narrative. The West Asia military conflict has disrupted global shipping routes and triggered Indian government gas rationing. For DEE, this means: (a) West Asia export shipments face Force Majeure delays — though the revised notice explicitly rules out contract cancellations and expresses confidence in fulfilling all obligations, (b) domestic O&G/petrochemical customers may slow project timelines due to gas supply constraints, and (c) working capital pressure intensifies as delayed shipments keep inventory and WIP locked up longer. Management has reaffirmed FY27 guidance in the revised notice, stating order book is "more than sufficient" to support the execution pipeline. The speed of the revision (within 24 hours) suggests management is attuned to investor sentiment and proactively managing the narrative.

The counterbalancing positive: India's energy supply constraints make coal-based thermal power more critical than ever. DEE's INR 1,100+ Cr power order book — which barely existed a year ago — is positioned on the right side of this macro shift. The crisis may paradoxically accelerate the very catalyst that drives DEE's re-rating.

The key risks to monitor are: (1) Working capital intensity — at 243 days CCC, the combination of rapid order book growth + export shipment delays could force liquidity measures. (2) Oil & Gas order replenishment — this segment saw near-zero inflow in February and now faces a macro headwind from gas rationing; if O&G doesn't recover, the order mix becomes single-sector dependent. (3) Duration of the Strait of Hormuz disruption — a short conflict is manageable, a prolonged one could defer INR 100-200 Cr of export execution into FY27. (4) The power segment tariff issue remains an overhang but the pellet plant pivot limits further damage.

With the updated order book, FY27 base case remains INR 1,800-2,200 Cr revenue with 18-20% core EBITDA margins, translating to INR 140-170 Cr PAT. The geopolitical situation introduces downside risk to the near-term (Q4 FY26 / Q1 FY27) execution trajectory but does not impair the structural thesis. The opening FY27 order book of ~INR 1,900-2,100 Cr provides substantially more visibility than earlier estimates assumed. The upcoming Anjar plant visit (March 20) could serve as a near-term sentiment catalyst.

**Bottom Line: DEE's thesis has two competing forces right now. The order book surge and thermal power conversion are the strongest proof points yet that the structural story is playing out. But the Strait of Hormuz disruption introduces execution uncertainty on exports and a macro headwind on O&G demand. The net read: the long-term thesis is strengthened, but the near-term path has gotten bumpier. The next watchpoints are: (a) resolution or escalation of the West Asia conflict, (b) O&G order replenishment in Q1 FY27, (c) analyst sentiment post the March 20 Anjar visit, and (d) Q4 FY26 execution pace — particularly whether domestic power orders can offset any export delays.**
